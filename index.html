2017 International Conference on Identification, Information and Knowledge in the Internet of Things

The Role of Science and Technology in Sport

Anton Kos, Yu Wei, Sašo Tomazic, and Anton Umek

Discover how wearable devices such as those from Garmin and Fitbit and smart sport equipment support motor learning through advanced sensor technology, real-time biofeedback, and machine learning in modern sport technology. Integration with the Internet of Things (IoT) allows seamless data collection and personalized feedback for athletes.

Abstract

Wearable devices, such as fitness trackers and smartwatches, have become part of daily life for many people. While these devices often provide basic data like step counts or heart rate averages, the demands in sports technology are much higher. Athletes require more detailed, accurate, and frequent performance monitoring to improve results and prevent injury. This paper introduces the concept of motor learning in sports and how technology can support it through real-time feedback and advanced data analysis. We explore the different types of sensors for sports tracking, as well as their strengths and limitations. Challenges in data collection and wireless communication—such as speed, accuracy, and computing power—are also discussed. Modern tools like machine learning for sports, data mining, and signal processing are presented as effective methods for interpreting complex athletic data. Finally, we look at current trends in smart sports equipment, real-time biofeedback systems, and the expanding role of the Internet of Things (IoT) in sports. These developments offer promising advances in wearable sports technology but also introduce new technical and practical challenges.
